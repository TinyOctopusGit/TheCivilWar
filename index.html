Causes of the Civil War

Samuel Styles, Nathan Wendell

  • 1850

    Compromise of 1850

    January 29, 1850

    Fugitive slave act was amended and slave trade in the capitol was abolished. California entered the union on the free state side. Created New mexico and Utah. Texas vs. New Mexico was settled.

    Sources

    "Introduction - Compromise of 1850: Primary Documents in American History - Research Guides at Library of Congress." https://guides.loc.gov/compromise-1850, Accessed 1 Jan. 1970.

  • 1850

    Fugitive Slave Act

    September 18, 1850

    The fugitive slave act states that all slaves must be returned to their owners even if they are in a free state. The Fugitive Slave Act was part of the Compromise of 1850.

    Sources

    "Fugitive Slave Act | American Battlefield Trust." https://www.battlefields.org, Accessed 1 Jan. 1970.


    Augustyn, Adam. "Fugitive Slave Acts" Britannica, 6 Jul. 2023, Https://www.britannica.com, Accessed 1 Jan. 1970.

  • 1852

    Publishing of Uncle Tom's Cabin

    March 20, 1852

    Harriet Beecher Stowe published Uncle Tom’s Cabin in 1852. The book was about a slave named Tom and the injustices of slavery. The book became so popular that Abraham Lincoln eventually met Stowe. The book allowed more Americans to become aware of how bad slavery was.

    Sources <4>Editors, History.com. ""Uncle Tom’s Cabin" is published" History, 13 Nov. 2009, https://www.history.com.
  • 1854

    Kansas-Nebraska Act

    May 30, 1854

    The Kansas-Nebraska Act was created by Stephen Douglas to try and make a route for a rail line to go. He created the territories, Kansas and Nebraska. The slave laws in these territories were ruled by popular sovereignty causing pro and anti slavery settlers to rush to Kansas to try and push the results in their favor. The conflict turned violent, causing what is called the Bleeding of Kansas.

    Sources

    "U.S. Senate: The Kansas-Nebraska Act."https://www.senate.gov

  • 1854-1861

    Bleeding Kansas

    War broke out between the 3 political parties. The pro-slavery, anti slavery and the free-staters. Psychological warfare, mayhem and violence were very common during this period. The issue settled when Kansas became a free state in the Union.

    Sources

    "Bleeding Kansas (U.S. National Park Service)." https://www.nps.gov/

  • 1857

    Dred Scott Case

    March 6, 1857

    The U.S. Supreme Court decided slavery was a right and that the Missouri Compromise was “unconstitutional.” It also said that African Americans had no rights and weren’t citizens. Nobody but the south liked the decision.

    Sources

    "Missouri Digital Heritage: Dred Scott Case, 1846-1857." https://www.sos.mo.gov

  • 1858

    Lincoln-Douglas debates

    Aug 21, 1858 - Oct 15, 1858

    The debates were seven debates between the senator Stephen A. Douglas and Abraham Lincoln for the seat on the senate representing Illinois. Lincoln lost, but this made him popular enough that he eventually became president.

    Sources

    "The Lincoln-Douglas Debates of 1858 - Lincoln Home National Historic Site (U.S. National Park Service)."https://www.nps.gov

  • 1859

    Raid on Harper's Ferry

    October 16, 1859

    John Brown tried to lead a revolt against a federal armoury in Harpers Ferry. His goal was to free the enslaved people and arm them so that they could fight back to destroy the institution of slavery. His revolt failed and he was hung for treason against the state of Virginia.

    Sources

    "John Brown's Harpers Ferry Raid | American Battlefield Trust" Battlefields, https://www.battlefields.org

    Histroy.com Editors, "John Brown’s raid on Harpers Ferry" History, 13 Nov. 2009, https://www.history.com

  • 1860

    Election of Lincoln

    November 6, 1860

    Lincoln won a 4-way competition for the presidency. He represented the Republican party and beat the Southern Democrats, the Democrats and the Constitutional Union.

    Sources

    Introduction - Presidential Election of 1860: A Resource Guide - Research Guides at Library of Congress." https://guides.loc.gov

  • 1860

    Secession of the Southern States

    December 20, 1860

    The Secession of the Southern States started with South Carolina passing an ordinance of secession. Mississippi, Florida, Alabama, Georgia, Louisiana, and Texas following suit in the next few months. President Lincoln sent troops to Fort Summer which was the final cause of the Civil War.

    Sources

    Britannica, The Editors of Encyclopaedia. "secession". Encyclopedia Britannica, 20 Apr. 2022, https://www.britannica.com/topic/secession.
